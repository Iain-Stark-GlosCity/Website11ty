Council Tax — your guide

Discounts based on who lives with you

Who lives at your address can reduce — or even eliminate — your Council Tax bill. These discounts are not means-tested and do not depend on your income. They are based entirely on the number and circumstances of the people at your home.

I live alone Someone in my home is a student
25% off if you are the only counted adult
50% off if all adults are disregarded
100% exempt if all residents are full-time students

Which situation fits you?

I live alone

You are the only adult at your address — or the only one who counts. You should be getting 25% off. Find out how to claim it or check your current discount is being applied.

Find out more →

Someone in my home is a student

A property occupied only by full-time students pays nothing. If you share with students, they may not count — which could mean you qualify for a discount.

Find out more →

Someone at my address doesn't count

Some people — including those with a severe mental impairment, unpaid carers, under-18s, and others — are disregarded when counting adults. This may reduce your bill even if you are not living alone.

Find out more →

My home is adapted for a disabled person

If your home has a qualifying adaptation — wheelchair space, an extra bathroom, or a room used for medical purposes — you pay one band lower than your actual band.

Find out more →

I am a care leaver aged 18 to 24

Care leavers of any English local authority who live in Gloucester may get 100% off their Council Tax bill until their 25th birthday.

Find out more →
