Ways to pay your Council Tax

Most people pay by Direct Debit — it's the easiest option and means you never miss a payment. You can also pay online, by phone, at a Post Office, or by cheque.

✓

Direct Debit is fastest to set up and gives you a choice of payment dates. Most people choose to pay in 10 monthly instalments from April to January.

Your payment options at a glance

Method When payment reaches us Best for
Direct Debit Automatically on your chosen date Most people
Online (My Gloucester) Same day (before 4pm) One-off payments
Phone (automated) Same day (before 4pm) Quick payments
PayPoint / Post Office 2–3 working days Cash payments
Bank transfer (BACS) 3 working days Businesses
Cheque by post Allow 5 working days If preferred

What you'll need

Whichever method you choose, you'll need your Council Tax account number. This is printed on your bill and on any letters we send you. It's usually 8 digits starting with a 5.

If you've lost your bill, sign in to your My Gloucester account to see your account number.

When payments are due

Your annual bill is usually split into 10 monthly instalments, payable from April through to January. If you miss an instalment:

  1. We'll send a reminder notice giving you 7 days to pay
  2. If you miss a second instalment, we may require you to pay the remaining balance in full
  3. If you are unable to pay, contact us immediately — the sooner you get in touch, the more options are available
📋

You can choose to pay in 12 monthly instalments (April to March) instead of 10. Contact us to change your payment schedule.
