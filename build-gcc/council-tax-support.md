## What Council Tax Support is

Council Tax Support (CTS) is a local scheme run by Gloucester City Council that reduces your Council Tax bill based on your financial circumstances. It is different from the national Single Person Discount — CTS is income-related and the amount varies.

  <div class="gcc-callout__body">
    <p><strong>You can claim CTS alongside other discounts.</strong> For example, if you also qualify for Single Person Discount (25% off for living alone), both reductions apply to your bill.</p>

## Who can apply

You may qualify for Council Tax Support if you:

- Are responsible for paying Council Tax at your address
- Are on a low income — from work, benefits, or a pension
- Have savings and capital below the scheme threshold
- Are not subject to immigration control (with some exceptions for certain groups)

People of **pension credit age** are covered by a separate nationally set scheme and may receive up to **100% support** in some circumstances.

Working-age applicants are assessed under Gloucester City Council's local CTS scheme. The maximum support is **80% of your bill** (after any other discounts).

## What affects how much you get

The amount of CTS you receive depends on:

- Your **net income** — earnings, benefits, pensions, and other income after deductions
- Your **savings and capital** — accounts over £6,000 may reduce your award; savings over £16,000 generally disqualify you
- **Who lives with you** — a non-dependant deduction applies if an adult who could be expected to contribute to housing costs lives in your home
- **Your household size** — more dependants generally means a higher award

## How to apply

Apply online via your [My Gloucester account](https://gloucester-self.achieveservice.com/). You'll need:

- National Insurance numbers for you and your partner (if applicable)
- Details of all income — wages, benefits, pensions
- Bank and savings account statements
- Details of anyone living with you

## If your circumstances change

You must tell us within **21 days** if your income, savings, or household change. Changes may increase or decrease your award. You can report changes through your My Gloucester account or by emailing **benefits@gloucester.gov.uk**.

## Appealing a decision

If you disagree with our decision, you have the right to:

1. Ask us to reconsider — write to us within **1 month** of the decision
2. Appeal to the independent Valuation Tribunal — within **2 months** of our reconsideration decision
